Cleaning the Dental Operatory

Select each step below to reveal detailed procedures and best practices for maintaining a safe clinical environment.

The Core Principle: Clean to Dirty

+

The entire cleaning process is governed by a single, critical principle: always work from the cleanest area to the most contaminated area. The rationale is to prevent transferring a heavy load of microorganisms from a dirty surface onto a cleaner one, which would defeat the purpose of cleaning.

Step 1: Preparation & Clearing the Area

+

Immediately after the patient leaves, the area must be safely cleared before the main cleaning can begin.

  • Practitioner's Role: The dentist or operator is responsible for removing and disposing of all sharps they used (needles, burs, scalpel blades). This is not the Dental Assistant's (DA's) role.
  • DA's Initial Tasks: While still wearing PPE from the procedure, the DA flushes suction lines and handpieces, then removes all reusable instruments into a rigid, lidded container for transport to the sterilisation area.
  • Discard Barriers: All single-use items and barrier wraps are removed and disposed of correctly.

Step 2: The Systematic Cleaning Sequence

+

After returning to the operatory and donning a fresh pair of gloves, the detailed wipe-down begins. Using a TGA-approved, pH-neutral detergent, clean the following surfaces in order, using a new wipe for each new area.

1
Overhead Light: Start with the handles and surfaces of the operating light.
2
Benchtops & Equipment: Wipe down all benchtops and small equipment like the X-ray head.
3
Dental Unit & Tubing: Clean the unit arms, bracket table, and all tubing for handpieces and suction.
4
Operator Chairs: Wipe the dentist's and assistant's chairs.
5
Patient Chair: Thoroughly clean the entire patient chair, including the headrest and armrests.
6
Spittoon & Suction: Finish with the most contaminated areas: the spittoon and suction connections.

Step 3: Correct Technique & Products

+
  • Wiping Method: Use an 'S' motion without lifting the cloth. This ensures the entire surface is covered effectively and prevents re-contaminating areas.
  • Products: Use a pH-neutral or mildly alkaline detergent. For soft upholstery on the dental chair, special products may be required to avoid damage. Always follow the chair manufacturer's instructions for use (IFU).
  • No Direct Spraying: Never spray cleaners directly onto a surface. This can aerosolise contaminants. Always apply the product to the cloth first.
  • Drying: After cleaning, all surfaces must be wiped dry with a clean, dry, lint-free cloth. A dry surface is less likely to support microbial growth.

Step 4: Safe PPE Removal (Doffing)

+

Safely removing PPE is the final step to prevent contaminating yourself or the clean environment. This must be done in a specific sequence.

  1. Remove Gloves: Peel off gloves without touching your bare skin with the contaminated outer surface. Discard.
  2. Perform Hand Hygiene.
  3. Remove Gown: Unfasten ties and roll the gown inside-out. Discard.
  4. Perform Hand Hygiene.
  5. Remove Eye Protection: Handle by the earpieces or headband only.
  6. Remove Mask: Grasp the ties or elastic bands. Do not touch the front of the mask. Discard.
  7. Perform Final Hand Hygiene.
